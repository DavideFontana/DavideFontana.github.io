OCULUS_MACHINA
INTERACTIVE INSTALLATION
2025_
< >

Oculus Machina shares a critical point of view towards artificial intelligence technology, it is an act of demystification pared with a satirical and provocative staging. A face recognition and profiling algorithm becomes a virtual fortune-teller, promising to read accurately into past, present and future of the analyzed face, generating prophecies not based on astrology or occult practices, but on pure mathematical deduction.

Since the AI boom, this technology seems to have become the answer to all our questions, the simplest and most convenient solution to our most complex and divisive problems. AI is surrounded by an aura of mysticism and obscurity that’s hard to cleave; a black box whose logic is incomprehensible even for the experts. This leads us to rely on it as a being of immeasurable power, capable of either guiding us towards a utopia or triggering the apocalypse, a digital oracle of absolute truth and justice. Tanks to this technology it’s not so absurd, after all, recognizing someone’s deeper feelings or identifying their sexual orientation just by letting a computer look a grainy picture of their face, or predicting who will commit a crime base on the way they walk, or again, making crucial military decisions on the war field, with hundreds of human lives on the line.

Often these implementations are pursued not because it’s reasonable to think that there may be any correlation between facial feature, and homosexuality, or criminal tendencies, for example, but because this is what is technically possible with machine learning. The ability to automate measurements like, skin color, eye distance, cranial size… (witch a computer can extract quickly and conveniently from a CCTV feed), stimulates the desire to find meaning in these approaches, even without any scientific proof, to then legitimise their validity on the AI superiority. (Kate Crawford, Atlas of AI, 2021)

"I worry that the current path of AI development, which relies heavily on the privileges of whiteness, men, and money, cannot produce an AI-mediated world of trust and compassion that serves the global majority in an equitable, inclusive, and accountable manner" -Stephanie Dinkins


Oculus Machina is inspired by the fortune-telling machines popularized in the early 1900, creating a provocative link between the pseudo-sciences related to mysticism, astrology, and even phrenology, and the latest AI algorithms.
